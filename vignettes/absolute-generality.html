La Vignetterie.

My thesis, explained.

I propose to explain a little of my thesis (Loo) in as close to plain language aspossible.

If I say that ‘everything is in the suitcase’, it is natural to think that‘everything’ is restricted (this example is drawn from Williamson). In this case,the domain of ‘everything’ is restricted, in a way determined by the context: wemight paraphrase the sentence as ‘everything mutually understood as to be packedhas been packed’, or similar. We call this phenomenon quantifier domain restriction.Linguists and philosophers of language are sometimes interested in how contextualdomain restriction works: when does it happen? what are the relevant restrictions?and so on (Stanley and Gendler Szabó). That is not what this thesis isabout.

In some cases, we might not think that the word ‘everything’ is intended to beread as restricted in the same way. For example: ‘everything is subject to the powerof God’; ‘science can discover the causal laws governing everything’; and ‘everythingis physical’.
